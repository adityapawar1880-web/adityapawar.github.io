Generative AI Content Experiment

Exploring the potential of AI to produce compelling marketing content efficiently and ethically.

Overview

In this self‑initiated project, I set out to test how generative AI could enhance marketing workflows. With the rapid development of AI tools, marketers must understand how to harness them responsibly. My goal was to evaluate the quality, performance and efficiency of AI‑generated creative against traditional approaches.

Approach & Responsibilities

  • Researched and selected leading AI models capable of generating text and imagery for marketing use cases.
  • Created a dataset of prompts and briefs that reflected common campaign scenarios, including product launches and seasonal promotions.
  • Generated 100+ pieces of copy and ad visuals using AI tools, refining prompts iteratively to optimise outputs.
  • Designed A/B tests comparing AI‑generated creative with human‑crafted versions across social media and email channels.
  • Collected performance metrics such as click‑through rate (CTR), engagement and time on page.
  • Documented insights and developed guidelines for ethical AI use, including transparency and bias mitigation.

Results & Impact

  • AI‑generated ads achieved a 15% higher CTR on average compared with traditional creative.
  • Production time for draft content decreased by over 50%, freeing up resources for strategy and analysis.
  • Demonstrated how AI can diversify content and inspire creative teams, while still requiring human oversight.
  • Developed a knowledge base that informed future AI initiatives at my organisation.

This experiment highlighted the importance of blending human creativity with machine assistance. Read more about my other marketing projects in the portfolio.
