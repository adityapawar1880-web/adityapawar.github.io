ThinkerPlace Research Capstone

Market research on the preferences of parents and children for DIY versus traditional toys, delivering actionable insights.

Overview

As part of my Bachelor’s programme, I led a research capstone for ThinkerPlace, a company offering educational DIY kits and toys. The project aimed to understand consumer perceptions and inform product development and marketing strategies.

Approach & Responsibilities

  • Designed and distributed surveys to parents and guardians to capture purchasing behaviour, preferences and pain points.
  • Conducted in‑depth interviews with children and educators to gauge the appeal of DIY kits versus ready‑made toys.
  • Analysed market data and competitor positioning using Excel and pivot tables.
  • Compiled findings into a comprehensive report, including segmentation analysis and customer personas.
  • Presented the results to ThinkerPlace stakeholders with visualisations, key takeaways and strategic recommendations.

Results & Impact

  • Identified a growing trend favouring educational DIY toys among parents seeking interactive learning experiences.
  • Recommended adjustments to ThinkerPlace’s product range and messaging that were subsequently adopted.
  • Provided insights that contributed to improved sales and stronger market positioning for the company.
  • Earned recognition from faculty for rigorous research methodology and clear communication.

This project strengthened my research and analysis skills and highlighted the importance of aligning products with consumer insights. Discover more work in my portfolio.
